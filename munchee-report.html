Section 01

Executive Summary

On 12 April 2026 the eve of Sinhala & Tamil New Year Munchee, Sri Lanka's number-one biscuit brand and flagship of the CBL Group, executed one of the most talked-about Awurudu activations in recent memory: a full sponsorship of every train ticket issued from Fort Railway Station for the entire day.

Nearly 25,000 passengers across 85 train departures travelled home free of charge, courtesy of Munchee. The campaign blended large-scale logistics with emotional storytelling, positioning Munchee not merely as a festive biscuit brand but as a brand that actively participates in the nation's most cherished cultural journey going home for Awurudu.

This report analyses the campaign's strategy, execution, reach, brand impact, and offers forward-looking recommendations for future seasonal campaigns.

Avurudu is Sri Lanka's most significant cultural celebration a time when millions travel back to their hometowns to celebrate with family. Train travel is the most common and culturally symbolic mode of transport for this journey, particularly for working-class and middle-income families. Fort Railway Station in Colombo serves as the primary departure hub for the entire country on this day.

Munchee has long been associated with Avurudu through product gifting and seasonal advertising. The 2026 campaign, however, elevated the brand's role from a passive participant in the festive table to an active enabler of the Avurudu journey itself.

Section 03

Campaign Strategy

Strategic Objective

The campaign's primary goal was to deepen emotional brand affinity by moving beyond transactional advertising. Instead of telling consumers that Munchee is part of Avurudu, the brand demonstrated it by removing one of the biggest practical barriers to homecoming: the cost of travel.

Core Insight

The human truth driving the campaign was simple and powerful: the Avurudu season is typically a time when people travel back to their hometowns, but with current economic conditions, travelling has become more challenging. By addressing a real consumer pain point affordability of travel rather than simply celebrating the season with imagery and jingles, Munchee created genuine relevance.

"The Avurudu season is typically a time when people travel back to their hometowns. However, with the current global conditions, travelling has become more challenging. In this context, as a brand that understands the needs and emotions of the people, Munchee is ready to offer a special experience for those arriving at Fort Railway Station on the 12th."

— Danushka Tamali Silva, GM Marketing, Munchee / CBL Group

Strategic Pillars

1. Mass Reach with Emotional Depth

  • Targeting the single highest-footfall day at the busiest station in Sri Lanka
  • Touching 25,000 individuals directly across all socioeconomic groups
  • Creating personal, memorable brand moments at the point of emotional peak

2. Cultural Authenticity

  • Aligning with Avurudu's core value of family reunion
  • Reinforcing Munchee's "Suba Rasaya of Awurudu" brand positioning
  • Going beyond product promotion to cultural participation

3. Nation-Unifying Symbolism

  • The Fort–Batticaloa train uniquely connects Colombo to the far east
  • Branded as the "Munchee Special Avurudu Journey" with onboard activations
  • Symbolised bridging both ends of the country under one brand

4. Earned Media Engine

  • A genuinely newsworthy act of generosity designed to generate organic coverage
  • Multiple national media outlets covered the campaign without paid placement
  • Social media virality amplified reach far beyond the 25,000 direct recipients
Section 04

Campaign Execution

Timeline & Activation

1
Pre-Campaign — Brand Announcement

Munchee announced the initiative via press releases to national media including Daily FT, Daily Mirror, and Newswire, building anticipation among the public and travellers planning their Avurudu journey.

2
12 April 2026 | Station Activation, Fort Railway Station

Munchee fully sponsored all 85 train departures from Fort Railway Station for the entire day. Every passenger arriving at the station received a free train ticket to their destination — no purchase necessary, no conditions.

3
12 April 2026 | The Munchee Special Avurudu Journey (Fort–Batticaloa)

The flagship activation: the Fort to Batticaloa train was transformed into a fully branded "Munchee Special Avurudu Journey." Passengers on this train were treated to onboard Avurudu games, refreshments, and gifts — creating a uniquely festive travel experience.

4
12 April 2026 | Station Giveaways

All passengers arriving at Fort Railway Station that day were given Munchee biscuit products — reinforcing the brand's position as an essential part of the Avurudu experience.

5
Post-Campaign | Media Amplification

National print, online, and social media outlets including Newswire, Daily FT, Daily Mirror, and their social channels carried extensive coverage. The campaign spread organically across social media, generating significant public goodwill.

Brand Impact Analysis

Reach & Visibility

The campaign generated multi-layered reach across three distinct audience tiers:

Tier Audience Estimated Exposure
Primary Direct beneficiaries — ticket recipients at Fort Station ~25,000 passengers
Secondary Fort Station bystanders, family members, station staff Tens of thousands
Tertiary Media audiences — print, digital, social media (LK & diaspora) Millions (national reach)

Emotional Brand Capital

The campaign's most significant achievement is the emotional equity it generated. By directly enabling Sri Lankans to reunite with their families at no cost, Munchee created a moment of genuine gratitude one of the most powerful drivers of brand loyalty. The act of giving without expectation of purchase created an authenticity that conventional advertising cannot replicate.

SWOT Assessment

Strengths

  • Massive, direct community impact — 25,000 beneficiaries
  • Perfectly aligned with Avurudu's cultural values
  • Significant earned media at near-zero paid media cost
  • Unique, hard-to-replicate brand ownership of the moment
  • Reinforces "Suba Rasaya of Awurudu" brand platform
  • Nation-unifying symbolism (Fort to Batticaloa)

Weaknesses

  • Limited geographic scope — only Fort Station, Colombo
  • One-day campaign with no ongoing consumer touchpoint
  • No digital/interactive consumer engagement layer reported
  • Product sampling alone may not drive measurable sales lift

Opportunities

  • Extend to multiple stations or other peak travel moments
  • Build a digital storytelling layer (passenger stories, UGC)
  • Create a recurring annual "Munchee Free Journey" tradition
  • Leverage for Tamil New Year inclusivity messaging

Threats

  • Competitor imitation in future Avurudu seasons
  • Operational complexity at scale — logistics risk
  • Public expectation management in subsequent years

Positioning Reinforcement

Munchee's long-standing Avurudu positioning — "Suba Rasaya of Awurudu" — has historically placed the brand on the festive table. The 2026 campaign dramatically expanded this positioning: Munchee is no longer just present at Avurudu; it is now the brand that brings you there. This is a significant evolution in brand narrative — from product presence to journey enablement.

Section 09

Recommendations for Future Campaigns

1
Make it an Annual Tradition — "Munchee Gedara Yanna" (Munchee Take Me Home)

Establish the free train journey as a recurring Avurudu institution. Building year-on-year consumer expectation creates compounding brand equity and deepens cultural ownership of the occasion. A named platform ("Munchee Gedara Yanna") would allow multi-year brand building.

2
Scale Geographically — Multi-Station Activation

Extend beyond Fort Station to key departure points in Kandy, Galle, and Jaffna. This would dramatically increase direct reach, reinforce the nation-unifying narrative, and create stronger earned media angles across regional news outlets.

3
Add a Digital UGC Layer — Passenger Story Campaign

Invite beneficiaries to share their homecoming stories on social media with a branded hashtag (e.g., #MuncheeGedaraYanna). Real passenger stories are among the most powerful forms of authentic content and would multiply organic reach significantly at minimal cost.

4
Deepen the Onboard Experience

The Munchee Special Avurudu Journey concept on the Batticaloa train is a best-in-class experiential touchpoint. Expanding this to multiple routes — with Avurudu games, cultural performances, and gift packs — would create richer documentary content and passenger word-of-mouth.

5
Measure Brand Sentiment & Sales Correlation

Implement pre/post brand health tracking (awareness, affinity, purchase intent) around the campaign window to quantify the brand equity return on investment. Correlate with point-of-sale data in the weeks following Avurudu to establish a business case for future investment.

6
Leverage the Tamil New Year Dimension

April 14 marks Thai Pongal / Tamil New Year — celebrated simultaneously. Future campaigns should explicitly embrace both communities, reinforcing Munchee as a brand for all Sri Lankans regardless of ethnicity, which would further strengthen the brand's unifying national narrative.

Section 10

Conclusion

The Munchee Avurudu 2026 Free Train Journey campaign is a masterclass in purpose-led, experiential, and culturally intelligent marketing. By identifying a genuine consumer pain point the difficulty and cost of Avurudu travel and directly solving it for nearly 25,000 Sri Lankans, Munchee moved beyond conventional seasonal advertising into the realm of brand action.

The campaign succeeded on every key marketing dimension: it generated massive direct reach, created deep emotional brand moments, earned significant organic media coverage, reinforced Munchee's long-standing Avurudu positioning, and created a national conversation all centred on a brand that does not just celebrate Sri Lankan culture, but actively participates in it.

For DC Group and the CBL marketing team, this campaign sets a powerful benchmark and a platform that can be built upon meaningfully in years to come. Munchee has earned its place — not just on the Avurudu table but on the journey home.
